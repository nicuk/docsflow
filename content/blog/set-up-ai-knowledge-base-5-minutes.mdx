You don't need a technical team to give your business an AI-powered knowledge base. Here's how to go from scattered files to instant answers in under 5 minutes.

Most businesses store critical knowledge across dozens of folders, drives, and inboxes. When someone needs an answer, they dig through files manually or ask the one person who "just knows." That approach breaks down fast as you grow.

An AI knowledge base solves this by letting anyone on your team ask questions in plain English and get accurate answers pulled directly from your documents. Below is the exact process to set one up with [DocsFlow](/docs), start to finish.

## Step 1: Sign Up and Create Your Workspace (30 Seconds)

Head to [DocsFlow](/#contact) and create your account. All you need is an email address. No credit card required.

During signup, you'll choose a custom subdomain for your workspace — something like `yourcompany.docsflow.app`. This becomes your team's dedicated URL for accessing the knowledge base. Bookmark it, share it, or add it to your company's internal tools page.

Once you confirm your email, you're in. The dashboard is clean and self-explanatory: a document library on the left, a search bar front and center.

## Step 2: Upload Your Documents (60 Seconds)

Click the upload area or drag and drop files directly into your workspace. That's the entire process.

DocsFlow supports the file types businesses actually use:

- **PDF** — contracts, reports, policies, invoices
- **DOCX** — SOPs, proposals, internal memos
- **XLSX** — financial data, inventory lists, pricing sheets
- **PPTX** — board decks, training materials, sales presentations
- **TXT and CSV** — logs, exported data, configuration files
- **Images** — scanned documents, receipts, whiteboard photos (OCR extracts the text automatically)

Upload one file or a hundred. There's no manual tagging, no folder structure to configure, and no preprocessing on your end. The AI parses each document automatically, extracting text, structure, and meaning so it's ready for search within seconds.

Start with whatever's most urgent — maybe your employee handbook, your top client contracts, or last quarter's board report. You can always add more later.

## Step 3: Ask Your First Question (30 Seconds)

This is where it clicks. Type a question into the search bar the same way you'd ask a colleague.

Here are a few examples that founders and operations managers typically start with:

- *"What's our refund policy?"*
- *"Summarize the Q3 board report."*
- *"What are the payment terms in the Acme contract?"*
- *"How many days of PTO do employees get after one year?"*

You don't need to know which document contains the answer. You don't need to remember file names or folder paths. The AI searches across everything you've uploaded and pulls the relevant information together into a direct response.

If you've used [RAG-based search](/blog/what-is-rag-plain-english-guide) before, this is the same underlying technology — made accessible without any technical setup.

## Step 4: Review Your Answer (Instant)

Every answer DocsFlow returns comes with built-in verification so you never have to take the AI's word for it.

**Source citations with page references.** Each answer lists exactly which documents it drew from, down to the specific page or section. If DocsFlow pulls from your vendor agreement on page 4 and your internal policy on page 12, you'll see both cited clearly.

**Confidence scores.** The AI indicates how confident it is in each response. High confidence means strong matches across your documents. Lower confidence signals that the answer may be partial or that you might want to review the source material.

**One-click verification.** Click any citation to jump directly to the relevant passage in the original document. You can read the surrounding context, confirm accuracy, and share the specific reference with a colleague — all without leaving the platform.

This transparency is what separates a useful AI tool from a risky one. You always know where answers come from.

## Step 5: Invite Your Team (60 Seconds)

A knowledge base is most valuable when your whole team can access it. From your workspace settings, invite team members by email.

DocsFlow uses **role-based access control** to keep information organized and secure:

- **Admin** — full access to all documents, settings, billing, and user management. Typically the founder, CEO, or operations lead.
- **User** — can search and ask questions, but only sees the documents they've been granted access to. Each person only sees what they should.

This means you can give your sales team access to pricing guides and proposals without exposing HR documents or financial records. Access is granular, and you stay in control.

Send the invite, assign a role, and your team member can start searching immediately from `yourcompany.docsflow.app`.

## What Happens Behind the Scenes

When you upload a document, DocsFlow parses its contents — text, tables, headings, and structure — and indexes everything into a searchable format optimized for AI retrieval. When someone asks a question, the system searches across all indexed documents, identifies the most relevant passages, and combines them into a coherent answer. Every answer links back to the original source material so nothing is generated without a foundation in your actual documents.

You don't need to understand any of this to use the platform. But it's worth knowing that the answers aren't invented — they're assembled from what your documents actually say.

## Tips for Getting the Best Results

A few small habits will dramatically improve the quality of answers your knowledge base returns.

**Use descriptive filenames.** Rename `doc_final_v3.pdf` to `Acme-Corp-Service-Agreement-2026.pdf` before uploading. The AI uses filenames as context, and clear names lead to better retrieval.

**Upload complete documents, not snippets.** A full contract gives the AI the context it needs to answer nuanced questions. A single extracted clause doesn't. When in doubt, upload the whole file.

**Be specific in your questions.** "What are the payment terms?" is good. "What are the net payment terms in the Acme Corp service agreement signed in January 2026?" is better. Specificity helps the AI narrow down exactly what you're looking for.

**Use the custom AI persona feature.** If your business uses specialized terminology — legal language, medical terms, construction jargon — configure the AI persona in your workspace settings to understand your industry vocabulary. This improves both search accuracy and answer quality.
